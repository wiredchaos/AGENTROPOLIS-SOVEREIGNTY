# Individual Sovereignty

Individual sovereignty is the foundational layer of AI sovereignty doctrine. All other sovereignty — of agents, organizations, and automated systems — derives its legitimacy from the rights and choices of human individuals.

---

## 1. The Individual as the Primary Sovereign

In AI-native systems, individuals are frequently:

- The source of training data
- The subjects of AI decisions
- The principals who authorize agents to act
- The consumers of AI-generated outputs
- The owners of personal memory and history

In each of these roles, the individual is the primary sovereign. Systems, agents, and organizations act in the individual's interest only when explicitly authorized, and are constrained by the individual's rights.

---

## 2. Individual Rights in AI Systems

### 2.1 The Right to Know
An individual has the right to know:
- What AI systems are acting on their behalf or about them
- What data those systems hold
- How that data is used
- What decisions or recommendations are made about them by AI

### 2.2 The Right to Consent
An individual has the right to:
- Consent before their data is collected, stored, or used
- Withdraw consent at any time
- Consent to AI acting on their behalf under specific, bounded conditions
- Refuse AI involvement in decisions that affect them

### 2.3 The Right to Access and Portability
An individual has the right to:
- Access all data held about them or generated by them
- Receive that data in a portable, standard format
- Transfer their memory and data to another system or provider

### 2.4 The Right to Correction and Deletion
An individual has the right to:
- Correct inaccurate information held about them
- Delete information they no longer wish to share (subject to lawful retention requirements)
- Request that AI models be retrained or adjusted to remove their data

### 2.5 The Right to Explanation
An individual has the right to a meaningful explanation of:
- Why an AI system made a decision affecting them
- What factors were considered
- What recourse is available if they disagree

### 2.6 The Right to Opt Out
An individual has the right to:
- Opt out of AI-assisted processes
- Require human review of AI decisions that significantly affect them
- Decline to be represented by AI in contexts where they prefer direct participation

## 3. Individual Control Over Agents

When an individual authorizes an AI agent to act on their behalf, they retain:

- The right to define the agent's scope of authority
- The right to view all actions the agent has taken
- The right to pause or terminate the agent at any time
- The right to approve or reject specific classes of action before they are taken
- The right to dispute actions taken by the agent

The agent acts in the individual's name but does not inherit the individual's full sovereignty. See [agent-sovereignty.md](agent-sovereignty.md).

---

## 4. Individual Memory Sovereignty

Personal AI memory — conversation history, preferences, behavioral patterns, emotional context — is among the most sensitive data in AI-native systems. Individuals have:

- **Primacy** over their own memory: they are the default owners
- **Transparency**: they can see what is remembered
- **Selective sharing**: they can share specific memories with specific systems under specific terms
- **Erasure**: they can delete memories from any system that holds them
- **Portability**: they can export their memories in a standard format

Memory held by a third-party system (e.g., a chatbot provider) is held in trust for the individual, not as the provider's property.

---

## 5. Identity Sovereignty for Individuals

Individuals should be able to:

- Choose how they are identified in digital systems
- Use multiple identities in different contexts (with appropriate separation)
- Verify their identity without disclosing unnecessary information
- Control which attributes are disclosed to which parties
- Revoke previously issued identity credentials

This is consistent with Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI) principles and does not require any specific technical implementation.

---

## 6. Individual Sovereignty vs. Collective Governance

Individual sovereignty is bounded by:

- The rights of other individuals (one person's sovereignty does not override another's)
- Lawful collective governance (laws, regulations, and court orders)
- The public safety boundary (see [public-safety-boundary.md](public-safety-boundary.md))

Individual sovereignty does not mean individuals can use AI to harm others, evade accountability, or undermine collective systems that protect individual rights.
